# 🏠 Nashville Housing Market Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end data analysis of the Nashville housing market using SQL and Power BI. The goal is to uncover trends in property sales, pricing behavior, and data quality issues while building a business-focused interactive dashboard.

---

## 🎯 Objective

To analyze housing market trends, identify key drivers of revenue, and evaluate data completeness to support better decision-making.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

* SQL (Data Cleaning & Analysis)
* Power BI (Dashboard & Visualization)
* DAX (Measures & Calculations)

## 🔍 Key Insights

* 📈 **Market Trend:** Property sales increased until 2015, followed by a noticeable decline, indicating a potential market correction phase.
* 💰 **Revenue Distribution:** Single-family properties contribute over 50% of total revenue, making them the dominant segment.
* 🏙️ **Geographic Concentration:** A large portion of transactions are concentrated in major cities, especially Nashville.
* 🛏️ **Bedrooms vs Price:** Property prices increase with bedroom count, but gains slow after 4–5 bedrooms (diminishing returns).
* ⚠️ **Data Quality:** ~46% of records contain missing values, highlighting limitations in data completeness.

---

## 🧠 Conclusion

The Nashville housing market demonstrates cyclical behavior with peak performance around 2015, followed by a decline in activity. The market is heavily driven by single-family homes and urban concentration, while data quality remains a key limitation for deeper analysis.
